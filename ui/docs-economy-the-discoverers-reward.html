The discoverer's reward

How much can a discoverer draw — and from where**?

Story context

  • Book ch 8 · Value & escrow
  • Book ch 11 · The break path
  • Book ch 14 · Bounties & pricing
  • Book ch 16 · Reputation signals

When a reproduced claim succeeds — independent re-audit confirms FAIL on pinned contract — discoverer is paid from escrow reserves. Never fresh mint.

Procedure: Vulnerability disclosure.

Payment formula

paid = min( bounty × boost,
            max( escrowBalance × discoveryCapBps / 10_000,
                 bounty × discoveryFloorBps / 10_000 ) )
ParameterDefaultMeaning
Boost subjectlastDiscoverer, else first PASS auditor; FAIL-at-audit = 1×Whose history sets boost
maxBoostFactor4Boost range 1×–5×
discoveryCapBps500Cap = 5% of general escrow
discoveryFloorBps5000Floor = 50% of bounty
claimFilingStake100 AUDITAt claim; refunded on success path
claimResolutionWindow30 daysExpire if stuck

Network subsidy ≈ bounty × (boost − 1) draws escrow first; locked bounty tops up if pot fixed at claim time.

Boost = reputation history — not assurance tier badges (Assurance tiers).

Deadline path

Claim expires without resolution → no pay; filing stake slashed. Liveness, not guilt — does not increment original auditor failed.
