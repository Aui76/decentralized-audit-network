3 · Accept your auditor

Available only while the audit is Assigned and you are its protocol. Accepting starts the auditor's review. If you do nothing, after the decision window anyone may auto-accept (advanceProtocolDecision) so it can't stall.

Accept your assignment (auditor)

For the assigned auditor. Re-run the spec check to get its errors root, then accept — that starts your review. Or decline within the window, no penalty. Available while the audit is Assigned and you are its auditor.

Default is the published value for withdraw-credits v1.1 — confirm it by running the spec tool yourself before signing.

Submit your verdict (auditor)

For the assigned auditor, after accepting. Run the tool on the contract to compute the result root, then submit pass or fail. Available while the audit is In review and you are its auditor.

4 · Confirm

5 · Open a dispute

If a bug is claimed (state Claimed) and you are the protocol, open a dispute re-audit on the original contract. Protocol-funded: the dispute bounty must be ≥ 50% of the original bounty (the cell enforces it). Two transactions: approve, then open.

File a bug claim (bug-hunter)

For a registered auditor who is not this audit's protocol or original auditor. Run a tool that fails on the contract to get its result root, then file. Available while the audit is Passed — window open, Audited, or Settled. Filing escrows a stake (the larger of 50% of the bounty or the floor). Two transactions: approve, then file.
