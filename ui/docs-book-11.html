The second outcome

Chapters 9 and 10 described pay when a pass survives. The break path is the opposite sign — same reproducible discipline.

A missed bug becomes a proof-bearing claim: FAIL `resultRoot`, stake, eligible party. That is the challenge (Gate C) — not we dislike the auditor, but reproducible fail on pinned code.

Without this path, pass would mean final at verdict. With it, pass means survived reproduction pressure until the window closed clean.

Why reserves, not issuance

Discoverer pay draws from reserves — treasury slices refilled by positive blocks. Self-attack cannot print money by cycling claims.

A fix audit on new bytecode may mint POS on its own row; discoverer on the original miss still comes from reserves.

Before you turn the page

Break path — missed bug → proof → reproduction → pay or slash.

Reserves — discoverer source; payer cannot veto.
