Assignment (CRD)

Who** gets assigned to an ordinary audit — and what stops protocols from stalling or farming the same auditor pair?

Story context

  • Book ch 9 · The audit cycle
  • Book ch 15 · The pool

Ordinary audits assign auditors by constrained random draw (CRD) over the pool — reject memory, dyad cap, FIFO fallback. Dispute / fix / gap / structural lanes use in-cell FIFO + exclusion — independence is structural, not draw-based.

Protocol-reject cap

A protocol cannot reject assigned auditors indefinitely.

min(MAX_PROTOCOL_REJECTS_CAP, 1 + successful/10), reduced by exploited-history penalty (floor 0). Budget scales with protocol track record — anti-griefing inside reject path.

High reject count → Bob SEQ-reject-loop — Participant phases (O3).

Multi-assignment

One auditor may hold multiple simultaneous assignments (ordinary + mid-dispute). _assignNext moves chosen auditor to queue tail but does not remove them. Decision windows and push-out bound non-response — not a settlement-soundness gap.
