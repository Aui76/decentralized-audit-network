One token, three movements

Where does AUDIT come from, where does it sit, and who gets paid** — in one pipeline?

Story context

  • Book ch 4 · The core model
  • Book ch 8 · Value & escrow

AUDIT moves through three settlement motions on every audit row:

MINT  →  HOLD  →  PAY

Three movements

MovementMeaningKey refs
MintNew supply on positive block (confirmed PASS) + secondary CAN/UPG pathsPositive block · Where mint splits
HoldBounty locked at submit; treasury slice fills reserves at confirmEscrow architecture
PayAuditor at confirm; discoverer from reserves on reproduced claimAuditor · Discoverer

Discoverer pay is never mint. Abandoned claims → deadline path (stake slash, no auditor failed).

The one rule

New supply: survived audit confirm, structural adoption (UPG), or tool canonization (CAN) — not discoverer payout. No discretionary inflation after genesis; minter locked one-way to IssuanceModule.

Signals (bands, tiers, phase tags) are not this pipeline — Value vs signal.
