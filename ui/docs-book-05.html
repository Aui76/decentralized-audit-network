Why definitions come first

Mechanics chapters tempt you to skim for escrow and minting. Skip this chapter and words start to blur: a “claim” sounds like a support ticket, “resolution” sounds like a committee.

The network uses those words narrowly. This chapter locks vocabulary before the machinery runs.

Proof-bearing claim

A claim is proof submitted under published rules. The pinned contract, a reproducible FAIL `resultRoot`, stake from an eligible party — all checked before the chain moves state.

OutcomeMeaning
Transaction succeedsMechanism accepted it
Transaction revertsNothing was accepted

There is no “under review” phase where rhetoric earns leverage.

Claimed

On-chain claimed names a state, not a tone. Acceptable proof landed that the pass was wrong, so the audit stops short of “finished” until the challenge procedure (break path) completes. The success path (positive path) cannot close while that challenge is active.

Pause in finality — not the opening act of a debate.

Resolution

There is no judge. After a claim is accepted, the network runs the challenge procedure (break path): independent re-run on the pinned artifact, match the filed FAIL `resultRoot`, pay or slash by rule, restore or fail the line. Silence → deadlines advance the same machinery — liveness, not mercy.

Think procedure, not court.
