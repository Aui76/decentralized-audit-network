Adaptive issuance — Uncapped Supply by choice

Story context

  • Book ch 10 · Issuance & positive blocks

What is not live yet · Appendix B

0. North star

Mint proves the mechanism is working — not that any individual audit was excellent. The issuance rate should reflect the network's genuine health as observed from its own signals, without a governor, without an admin key, and without fixed parameters that require human judgment to calibrate at deploy time.

This document describes how the network achieves that by reading its own health at every scale simultaneously and adjusting issuance accordingly.

1. Why uncapped supply and why it must be adaptive

A fixed supply token has a predetermined equilibrium. It distributes what it has and waits for the environment to assign value. It cannot change its own behavior in response to what it observes. It is a closed thermodynamic system — it tends toward maximum entropy and has no internal mechanism to do further work once its supply is spent.

This network is a battlefield with no external protector. It requires ongoing incentives to fund its own defense — against exploits, against lazy auditors, against coordination attacks. A fixed supply that exhausts its incentive budget has no recourse.

Uncapped supply is the capability to respond. But an uncapped supply that responds to all signals equally becomes gameable — a single large coordinated injection can push the issuance rate wherever an attacker wants. The design challenge is: make the system light for genuine state transitions and heavy for manufactured ones.

The mechanism below achieves this by building issuance on top of signals the network already computes about its own participants.

2. The coordinate space (foundation)

The full theoretical grounding is in Network flow (health book). Summary for issuance purposes:

Value-agnostic principle: valueSecured is self-reported and unverified. The network does not use it anywhere in its own mechanics and does not use it here. D3 uses bounty — committed, on-chain capital.

3. The fractal health framework

The network's health is observable at every scale from the same framework:

Individual audit
    → hint tools (PASS/FAIL per axis)
    → card.flow (D3 magnitude + thermodynamic cluster signals)

Participant
    → phase tags: unproven · alive · declining · detrimental
    → maturity band: cold · warm · hot

Cluster
    → FLOW hint (isolation coefficient + temporal coherence)
    → communicating vessels — sealed or open?

Network
    → aggregate phase distribution across all active participants
    → networkHealth index (computable from indexer)

Each level reads the level below it. The network's health is the sum of its participants' phases. No new data is needed — just aggregation of what the indexer already computes.

Network health index:

networkHealth = (aliveAuditors / totalActiveAuditors)
              × (aliveProtocols / totalActiveProtocols)
              × (steadyLines / totalActiveLines)

Near 1.0: participants are in good standing, lines in good trajectory. Trending toward 0: the distribution is shifting into declining and detrimental territory.

This index is behavior-based, not calendar-based — consistent with the maturity ontology's core principle. Entropy enters only through documented failure paths.

4. The dual EMA

Two exponential moving averages run simultaneously:

emaFast  — high emaWeightBps (~2000–3000)  — tracks recent signal closely
emaSlow  — phase-weighted, clean signal only (see below)

4.1 emaFast

Updated after every confirmed completion (InBlock or Audited). All completions contribute. This is the raw pulse of network activity.

4.2 emaSlow — phase-weighted, clean signal

Updated after confirmed completions but with two filters:

Filter 1 — thermodynamic cleanliness (deferred on-chain): FLOW-FAIL completions (sealed vessel, suppressed timing) are excluded from emaSlow updates. The EMA of healthy signal is built from genuinely healthy signal only.

Filter 2 — participant phase weighting (deferred on-chain; proxy live):

Participant phaseWeight in emaSlow updateReasoning
alive1.0Full signal — these are who we trust
unproven0.25Low weight — a sybil influx during depression must not inflate the clean baseline; 0.25 caps this attack surface while still allowing the baseline to build in early network life
declining0.1Near-zero — declining participants degrade the health signal; they barely register in network memory
detrimental0.0Excluded — no contribution to clean baseline

Why unproven is 0.25 not 0.5: During a depression, a flood of new unproven registrations is suspicious — it may be a wash ring registering fresh addresses to inflate emaSlow and trigger a larger floor supplement. 0.25 closes this attack surface without preventing genuine new entrants from eventually contributing once they become alive.

The effective update weight for a completion is the minimum of the auditor and protocol phase weights. A declining auditor on an alive protocol contributes at 0.2. A detrimental auditor contributes nothing.

Filter 3 — protocol credibility weighting (testnet): The bounty scalar fed into the emaSlow update is credibility-weighted by the submitting protocol's submission history (kProtocol on-chain). A fresh protocol address blends toward the network mean bounty; an established protocol's own bounty level is taken at face value.

protocol_credibility_bounty = Z_protocol × protocol_mean_bounty
                             + (1 − Z_protocol) × network_mean_bounty

Z_protocol = N_submissions / (N_submissions + K_protocol)   [K_protocol = 10]

This closes the attack surface where a fresh protocol address inflates emaSlow with an artificially large bounty, independent of the thermodynamic wash-ring detection that operates at the cluster level. Protocol credibility operates on individual addresses; FLOW signals operate on clusters. Both are needed.

Note: protocol credibility affects only the emaSlow bounty input. Raw posted bounty still determines auditor and discoverer payouts — those calculations are unchanged.

What emaSlow represents (design intent): The network's memory of what genuine healthy activity looks like — FLOW-clean, phase-weighted, credibility-blended. On 84532, protocol credibility (Filter 3) and the auditor successful-count proxy are live; FLOW exclusion and full phase weights are not yet on-chain.

4.3 The divergence signal

divergence = emaFast / emaSlow
DivergenceReading
≈ 1.0Stable — both timescales agree
>> 1.0Fast growing faster than clean baseline — potential manipulation or genuine growth
<< 1.0Fast falling below clean baseline — depression

The direction of divergence combined with thermodynamic signals determines the network state (§5). On 84532, issuanceNetworkState() classifies from fastRatioBps = emaFast × 10000 / emaSlow only — not FLOW or aliveRatio.

5. The four network states

Design conditions below include FLOW and phase context. Live state machine: issuanceNetworkState() — Manipulation when fastRatioBps > manipulationThresholdBps; Depression when fastRatioBps < depressionThresholdBps; Recovery when emaFast > lastEmaFast and ratio below 10000; else Stable.

State 1 — Stable health

activityMint = emaSlow × emaToMintBps / 10000              (emaToMintBps = 2500)

             : min(activityMint, mintLpCapBps × lpBalance / 10000)   (mintLpCapBps = 500, LP-pace smoother)
  • No S_max, no supplyTailBps, no emaBlendBps. Manipulation damping (State 2) still applies on top.
  • Genesis = the first ordinary audit (submitGenesisAudit, declared-unfunded B_g); first mint = formula(B_g) ≈ 312 at B_g = 5,000.

State 2 — Suspected manipulation

Live: Mint still derives from emaSlow (activity curve); when fastRatioBps > manipulationThresholdBps, reward is scaled by manipulationMintScaleBps (default 8000 = 80%). The fast EMA does not replace the slow basis — it triggers a discount. Design intent: quarantine inflated fast signal via phase-weighted emaSlow; full quarantine awaits on-chain phase + FLOW filters (§4.2).

State 3 — Depression

See §6 for the depression floor mechanism in full.

State 4 — Recovery

Live: issuanceNetworkState() returns Recovery when emaFast > lastEmaFast and fastRatioBps < 10000. Design adds FLOW PASS confirmation — indexer-only on 84532. Trust rising emaFast immediately; no manipulation discount applies while ratio is below the manipulation threshold.

6. The depression floor

6.1 Detecting depression

Depression is not a binary switch. It is a gradient:

depressionIntensity = max(0, 1 − (emaFast / emaSlow))

Zero when both EMAs agree. Approaches 1.0 when recent activity collapses relative to clean baseline. Floor contributions are proportional — gentle when depression is mild, full when deep.

6.2 Treasury first, fresh mint as backstop

CellEscrow (referenced as treasuryEscrow in IssuanceModule) accumulates 24.9% of every mint during healthy periods (see §6.6 for derivation). That accumulation is the oil reserve — saved during good times, deployed during bad ones.

Order of operations during depression:

  1. Normal mint formula produces totalReward
  2. Floor supplement = emaSlow × floorFraction × depressionIntensity
  3. Pay floor supplement from CellEscrow escrow balance first, up to maxEscrowDrawdownPerAudit
  4. If escrow balance insufficient: pay remainder from fresh mint (see §7 for conditions)
  5. maxEscrowDrawdownPerAudit = 0.3% of current escrow balance per confirmed completion (geometric drain — see §6.6)

Escrow-funded floor supplement does not increase total token supply. Fresh-mint supplement does. The system minimizes supply dilution by exhausting reserves first.

Critical property — one-way drain during depression. Floor supplements are paid from escrow, not from new mint. Since they are not new mint, no treasury split occurs on them — meaning no 24.9% flows back into escrow from floor payments. During depression, mint collapses (emaFast → 0), treasury accumulation → 0, and every floor payment is a pure one-way draw with no return path. The escrow must arrive at depression onset already carrying the full load it needs to survive the depression. It cannot refill itself while running.

6.3 Behavioral expiry — two signals, no calendar

The floor cannot run forever, but when it stops should not be decided by a timer. Calendar time is not a behavioral signal. The maturity ontology's principle applies here too: entropy enters only through documented failure paths, not through birthdays.

The floor decays from two independent behavioral observations, whichever is faster:

Signal A — failed recovery attempts (live).

When recovery conditions are satisfied but recovery isn't happening, the problem is structural, not liquidity. Count how many times the floor has been active with green-light proxies met but emaFast has not risen:

failedRecoveryAttempts++ when (on-chain, at confirm):
  fastRatioBps < depressionThresholdBps
  AND fastRatioBps >= recoverabilityFactorBps
  AND emaFast <= prevFast
  AND floorDecayBps() > 0

Design intent also requires FLOW PASS and aliveRatio > healthBaseline × recoverabilityFactor — not checked on-chain today (§7 F-64 proxies).

Signal B — healthBaseline itself declining (deferred).

If healthBaseline — the EMA of aliveRatio during clean periods — is itself trending downward, the network's own definition of healthy has shifted. The floor is trying to maintain a level that no longer exists as the network's normal. The floor decays proportionally:

baselineDecay = currentHealthBaseline / peakHealthBaseline

As the baseline drifts lower, the floor pays out less. The system has accepted — from its own signals — that the world has changed. Not because a timer expired. Because the network's memory of healthy has been revised downward through documented behavioral history.

Combined decay (design):

floorDecay = min(
  max(0, 1 − (failedRecoveryAttempts / maxFailedAttempts)),
  baselineDecay
)
effectiveFloor = baseFloor × depressionIntensity × floorDecay

Live: floorDecayBps() implements Signal A only (linear decay to zero at maxFailedRecoveryAttempts). Supplement amount is not multiplied by floorDecay on-chain — decay gates green-light mint via _greenLightMintAllowed, not floor payout size.

maxFailedAttempts is a behavioral count — how many times do we try before accepting the recovery isn't coming. It is not a duration. It counts documented failures, not elapsed time.

6.4 Who benefits from the floor

Design intent (mainnet target): floor supplement applies only to confirmed completions by alive participants. declining and detrimental participants receive no floor supplement — only their normal (discounted or zero) weight in the formula.

Testnet today (84532): _updateEmaAndPayFloor pays the completing auditor whenever depression intensity triggers; there is no on-chain AuditorPhase check. emaSlow uses a successful-count proxy (emaSlowUnprovenWeightBps), not Bob alive / declining tags. See What is not live yet.

This matters for two reasons. First: it prevents the floor from rewarding the actors who contributed to the depression. Second: it creates a selection effect — auditors who continue working honestly through a depression are revealed as committed participants.

6.5 The loyalty premium — survival instinct as the economically dominant strategy

The floor makes staying survivable. The loyalty premium makes staying the best long-term choice even when exit looks rational in the short term.

The reflexivity risk without this. If staying during depression is merely survivable but not advantaged at recovery, then when participants correctly read the floor signals and model their recovery position, leaving and returning later is neutral or better. This is the bank run dynamic: individually rational exit, collectively catastrophic.

The mechanism. Track a depressionParticipation counter per auditor: confirmed completions while depressionIntensity > 0.3. This counter is visible on the Bob card and accumulates throughout the depression period.

During State 4 (recovery), the emaSlow phase weight for an alive auditor with nonzero depressionParticipation is 1.2 instead of 1.0. Their completions contribute more to the clean baseline during recovery. They earn more from the same work because their prior commitment is recognized.

recoveryWeight = 1.0 + (0.2 × min(1, depressionParticipation / participationThreshold))

This caps at 1.2 to prevent gaming via spam completions during mild depressions. participationThreshold is the number of completions needed to earn the full 1.2 multiplier — proposed as 5 confirmed completions during depression (meaningful participation, not a single audit).

What this closes. An auditor who exits during depression and returns during recovery earns the same as any other new or returning participant — 1.0 weight. An auditor who stayed earns 1.2 for the entire recovery period. The rational strategy is not exit-and-return. It is stay. Survival instinct is no longer noble but economically unprofitable. It is the dominant strategy.

6.6 Calculated escrow parameters

These three parameters were derived from the same behavioral framework as §9 — not set arbitrarily.

The escrow equilibrator principle. Escrow is not a savings account. It is the mechanism that equilibrates network supply (audits, verdicts, tool primitives, structural fixes) with participant demand (reasons to stay over the alternative of no escrow at all). At the wrong level it fails in one of two directions: too high and escrow capture becomes the dominant participant strategy (manufacturing vulns, triggering depressions, farming structural upgrades); too low and escrow is too thin to change any behavior — the counterfactual of no escrow produces the same decisions.

The right level is the behavioral threshold: the minimum that tips the marginal participant's calculus across all four draw types simultaneously — depression floor, discoverer payouts, structural upgrade tranches, integrity review subsidies — without making any of them the primary optimization target.

Escrow serves four draw types, not one. Calculating the accumulation rate from depression floor alone understates the needed rate. All four draws compete for the same pot:

Draw typeFrequencyAvg escrow drawExpected draw per audit
Depression floor20% of audits0.45 × normalMint × avg intensity 0.50.045 × normalMint
Discoverer payouts~3% of audits~1.5× bounty escrow boost0.045 × normalMint
Structural upgrades~1 per 150 audits~8 × normalMint (two tranches)0.053 × normalMint
Integrity review subsidy~1 per 25 audits~0.5 × normalMint0.020 × normalMint
Total equilibrium draw rate~0.163 × normalMint

Self-funding correction. During depression, mint collapses → treasury accumulation → 0 → escrow inflow → 0. The escrow is a one-way drain during the exact period it runs hardest. Accumulation happens only during healthy periods. Recalculating over 1,000 total audits (800 healthy, 200 depression):

Accumulation:      800 × R × normalMint
Healthy draws:     800 × 0.118 × normalMint   (discoverer + structural + integrity)
Depression draws:  200 × 0.225 × normalMint   (floor at avg 0.5 intensity)

To reach minimum useful depth (60 × normalMint) by end of 1,000 audits:
800R = 199.4  →  R = 24.9%

Non-negligible bound: at 24.9%, expected escrow value per audit ≈ 16% of normalMint — above the ~10% threshold where participants factor it into behavior. ✓

Not-dominant bound: 16% is far below the ~50% threshold where escrow optimization crowds out genuine audit work. ✓

**treasuryEscrowSplitBps = 2490** (24.9% of treasury share per block to escrow; remaining 75.1% to LP). Replaces the prior arbitrary 20/80 split.

**maxEscrowDrawdownPerAudit = 0.3% of current escrow balance (30 bps)**

Expressed as a fraction of escrow balance rather than a fixed amount, so drain is geometric — each draw reduces the base, the next draw is smaller. This makes depletion timing nonlinear and impossible for participants to model precisely from observable state alone.

At 0.3% per audit, halving the escrow takes ~230 audits (ln(0.5)/ln(0.997)). At ~10 depression audits per failed-recovery-attempt period, halving takes 23 periods. Since maxFailedAttempts = 25, behavioral expiry arrives before the escrow can halve from drawdown alone. The floor expires before the reserve runs dry. This is the required ordering.

**escrowMinimumThreshold = 3.6 × emaSlow**

Equivalent to 8 audit-equivalents of full floor intensity (8 × 0.45 × emaSlow). Fresh mint activates only when the remaining escrow can cover fewer than 8 more depression audits at current baseline — genuinely depleted, not merely low.

Expressed in emaSlow rather than fixed tokens so the threshold scales automatically with network activity. A growing network has a higher threshold in absolute terms but the same meaning: 8 audits of runway. Denominating in a continuously-updating signal (emaSlow) makes the threshold-crossing time self-referential — participants cannot model it without knowing future emaSlow, which depends on their own participation decisions.

7. The mint green-light — crossing from escrow to fresh mint

Fresh mint during depression is a bet on recovery. It dilutes existing holders. The system makes this bet only when its own signals support it.

All four conditions must be true:

1. escrow balance < escrowMinimumThreshold               (reserves genuinely depleted)
2. aliveRatio > healthBaseline × recoverabilityFactor    (enough alive to recover)
3. FLOW signals mostly PASS                              (depression genuine, not manufactured)
4. floorDecay > 0                                        (floor has not behaviorally expired — §6.3)

Testnet (F-64): condition 2 → fastRatioBps >= recoverabilityFactorBps; condition 3 → emaFast > lastEmaFast. Not aliveRatio or FLOW PASS. Mainnet (deferred — sprint item 20): replace proxies per `green-light-real-gates-deferred.md. See greenLightMintAllowed()` and F-64.

Condition 1 — snapshot timing (implementation note)

On each depression confirmAudit, _updateEmaAndPayFloor reads **escrowBal once** before payFloorSupplement:

  1. escrowBal → compute drawdownCap and supplement
  2. payFloorSupplement drains escrow (returns paid ≤ supplement; F-32)
  3. Green-light shortfall mint runs only if paid < supplement and escrowBal < threshold (same pre-payment snapshot)

So condition 1 compares escrow balance as it was at the start of that confirm's floor step, not the balance after the floor draw. In the same transaction, on-chain CellEscrow.escrowBalance() can be lower than the escrowBal used for the threshold check.

EffectTypical magnitude
Green-light may mint when live escrow already crossed thresholdAt most one floor payment of lag per confirm
Threshold check may use a slightly higher balance than post-draw realitySame — one-step stale

Policy (v1): Accepted edge case — usually negligible because the threshold is very low (8 audit-equivalents of full floor) and drawdown is capped per confirm (0.3% of balance). Not a revert risk — worst case is mint/backstop timing off by one confirm's floor step.

Integrators: greenLightMintAllowed() does not encode condition 1 (escrow depletion); it only reflects §7 conditions 2–4 proxies. For "is escrow below threshold?" use live escrowMinimumThreshold() vs CellEscrow.escrowBalance() after the block, not the in-call snapshot.

Deferred: Re-read escrowBalance() after payFloorSupplement before the threshold compare (mainnet hardening).

Condition 2 is the critical gate.

If aliveRatio has dropped below healthBaseline × recoverabilityFactor, the network has already gone structurally compromised. Minting into a network where most participants are declining or detrimental does not fund recovery — it funds the final extraction. The system recognizes this and stops.

If condition 2 is satisfied but condition 3 is not (FLOW FAILs rising during the depression), the depression is manufactured — someone is suppressing activity to trigger the floor while running extraction on the side. The system does not mint into this condition.

Condition 4 replaces the old maxFloorDuration calendar check. The floor has not expired when there are still behavioral recovery attempts available and the baseline has not shifted so far that the floor amount would be negligible anyway. This is a behavioral gate, not a timer. See §6.3.

Only when all four conditions are met does fresh mint activate as a survival mechanism — and at a reduced rate relative to healthy-period issuance, signaling distress to the market rather than hiding it.

8. The adaptive baseline — the network knowing itself

8.1 healthBaseline

healthBaseline = emaSlow of (aliveRatio)
                 computed only during periods where:
                   — networkHealth > healthyThreshold
                   — FLOW mostly PASS across active audits
                   — escrow balance growing (treasury is accumulating, not depleting)

This is the network's memory of what good health felt like for it specifically. A vibrant network that historically ran at 85% alive participants has a different baseline than a network that has always operated lean at 55%.

8.2 Why this dissolves the parameter problem

All thresholds that previously required human calibration at deploy time now derive from the network's own history:

Previously fixed parameterNow derived from
Depression thresholdhealthBaseline × depressionFactor
Floor amountemaSlow × floorFraction (relative to clean baseline)
Mint green-light alive thresholdhealthBaseline × recoverabilityFactor
EMA slow weightImplicit in phase weighting — behavior-derived

The system calibrates its thresholds against what healthy looks like for it. A vibrant network protects itself more aggressively. A lean network has lower absolute thresholds but they are equally meaningful relative to its own normal.

8.3 Why it cannot be gamed

The baseline builds only during verified clean periods — FLOW PASS, escrow growing, networkHealth above threshold. To manipulate the baseline upward, an attacker would need to maintain genuinely clean thermodynamic signals across the entire baseline accumulation window. That requires doing real work at scale for an extended period. The cost approaches the cost of simply being a legitimate participant.

8.4 Seasonal and cyclical memory

The slow EMA incorporates the network's own activity cycles. If the network always sees lower activity during bear markets, the baseline reflects that. A seasonal dip does not look like a crisis because the baseline already knows what that period normally feels like. The system distinguishes "slow but normal for this time" from "genuinely in trouble."

9. Calculated behavioral ratios

The design has no parameters that require human judgment to be meaningful. What remains are behavioral ratios — proportional relationships between signals the network already computes. Their values are derived from two principles: survival instinct must be the economically dominant strategy, and correct signal reading must not trigger reflexive collective exit.

The design is fully self-calibrating from its own behavioral history. No calendar. No external reference. No admin key required to adjust when conditions change.

11. Relationship to existing tokenomics proposals

tokenomics-proposals.txt defines the baseline mechanism: EMA-smoothed bounty-linked issuance with a hybrid curveAnchor + emaLine formula, uncapped supply, and a quality package (staged mint, slashable bonds, assurance tiers Phase A shipped — Standard/High at submit, tier windows + claim stake mult; bonds/staged mint/second review deferred per `../../proposals/applied/assurance-tiers-proposal.txt`).

This document does not replace that baseline. It sits on top of it:

  • The bountyEma in tokenomics-proposals.txt §3 becomes emaSlow here — phase-weighted and thermodynamically filtered (full filtering deferred; see §4.2)
  • The built v1 on 84532 uses emaToMintBps only (+ mintLpCapBps LP smoother) — not the archive curveAnchor hybrid. Hybrid blend remains deferred.
  • The quality package (§8 there — staged mint, bonds, second review) is orthogonal to and compatible with this framework
  • emaBlendBps / supplyTailBps from the archive were dropped (tail redundant; see bounty-ema-hybrid-mint-deferred.md)

12. Open questions

Calculated values are in §9. Remaining open questions:

  • **maxEscrowDrawdownPerAudit: Calculated — 0.3% of current escrow balance per audit (§6.6).** Geometric drain; behavioral expiry arrives before escrow halves under normal depression audit rates.
  • Phase weighting — protocol vs auditor: Use min(auditorWeight, protocolWeight) as proposed, or geometric mean? Min is conservative and harder to game (attacker must compromise both sides). Geometric mean is smoother but easier to partially game. Recommendation: min for the same reason the network uses strict exclusions elsewhere.
  • **baselineDecay floor:** Should baselineDecay have a minimum value (e.g. 0.1) so the floor never fully zeroes from baseline drift alone, leaving Signal A (failedRecoveryAttempts) as the final gate? Full zero is more honest about permanent state change. Minimum floor is more conservative but risks maintaining a zombie floor. Proposed: allow full zero from either signal — honesty about permanent state change is more important than conservative caution.
  • **participationThreshold calibration (loyalty):** 5 confirmed completions during depression proposed. Too low = gameable by anyone who submits a few audits. Too high = excludes participants who tried but had audits fail. Validate against typical auditor completion rates.
  • Loyalty multiplier duration: Does the 1.2 recovery multiplier apply for the entire recovery phase until emaFast ≈ emaSlow, or for a fixed window after recovery begins? Entire recovery phase is proposed — the multiplier fades naturally as recovery completes and State 1 (stable) is reached.
  • Interaction with staged mint (§8.1 of tokenomics-proposals.txt): The deferred portion of staged mint — does it also receive floor supplement treatment or only the immediate portion? Likely immediate only; deferred is already a bet on future non-exploit. Loyalty multiplier applies to immediate portion only for the same reason.
