Rewards

Who gets paid, when, and from where — and why the token only exists because real work was done.

Paid at confirmation

An auditor isn't paid when they submit a verdict — only at confirmation, after the challenge period closes with no bug found. That's the stake: the reward depends on the contract surviving scrutiny, not just on a verdict being filed.

What an auditor earns

A confirmed pass pays the bounty back from escrow, plus a freshly minted block reward. That mint follows network activity — busier periods mint more per block, subject to liquidity pacing — not a “early network earns more because supply is empty” curve. A dispute re-auditor earns the dispute bounty for re-checking the original contract, with no fresh mint.

Treasury slice

Each block also mints a matching treasury share: most lands in LP liquidity; the rest timelocks into escrow reserves that fund discoverers and floor supplements. See Tokenomics for the full split.

Where the tokens come from

New tokens enter circulation only through confirmed audits. Every token in existence is the record of a real audit that survived the challenge period — the network funds itself from its own work, not from anyone's goodwill.

Reserves and discoverers

Part of each reward feeds a reserve. When a bug is reproduced on a contract, the finder is paid from that reserve — never from new supply. Failures draw down reserves; they are never rewarded with fresh tokens.
