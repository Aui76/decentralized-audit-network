The positive block

When does fresh mint happen for an auditor — and what number** is authoritative after confirm?

Story context

  • Book ch 10 · Issuance & positive blocks
  • Book ch 22 · Reading the chain

New AUDIT enters circulation when `IssuanceModule` pays a confirmed positive block — one audit that survived challenge window and `confirmAudit`.

Design rationale: Adaptive issuance — Uncapped Supply by choice. Live formula: Activity-led reward.

What counts as a positive block

PropertyMeaning
SurvivedBinary PASS + challenge window elapsed + confirmAudit executed
Block sizeOne (`BS = 1`) — each successful audit is its own mint anchor
Supply capUncapped — no remaining-supply clamp on this line

Locked minter

Genesis wires minter → IssuanceModule, then `lockMinter()` one-way. After lock:

Allowed mintNot allowed
POS confirm, UPG adopt, CAN canonizationAdmin discretionary inflation
Discoverer pay (reserves only)

Preview vs actual (critical for integrators)

Auditor is not paid at verdict. Payment waits until window ends and confirm runs.

After confirm, always auditBlockRewardMinted(id) — never replace with a fresh preview (Settlement vs signal).
