Signal-to-action rule

An objective on-chain fact may gate pay or punish. A derived signal may only suggest. No off-chain signal — however confident — auto-slashes, auto-pays, or auto-voids.

The gap between "an indexer thinks X" and "the mechanism does X" is deliberate. Doctrine: Audit mutex § triangle-alarm.

Indexer suggests  →  Human or integrator chooses  →  On-chain fact gates settlement

Why they stay off-chain

Signals are derived and subjective. Gating settlement on them would let a biased or buggy indexer move money. A bad organ can mislead; it cannot mis-settle. The cell never reads a signal — only the facts it stores.

That is what makes the organ market permissionless and replaceable.
