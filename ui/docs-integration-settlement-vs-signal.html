Settlement vs signal

Which card fields move AUDIT** — and which only orient attention?

Story context

  • Book ch 16 · Reputation signals

Strangers see one surface — bounty, expected reward, tier badges, band labels — and often read those as price. Settlement is what contracts transfer. Signal is what indexers derive for cards. The chain never reads signal back.

Settlement rules live in the AUDIT Economy (mint, escrow, payout). This book covers display — how organs label rows without becoming wallet inputs.

Economy fence post: Value vs signal — settlement vs advisory at the ledger. This chapter: integrator discipline for card fields (bands, tiers, ripe-slot).

Economic signals in this book

Part IV names two signals that look like economics but sit outside the ledger. Both are rebuilt from public audit data each tick; neither appears in a transfer or revert.

Bounty bands answer a cohort question: where does this locked bounty rank against other audits that still have money on the line? An indexer collects economically active bounties, computes quartiles, and classifies each row as top_quartile, mid, or low. That rank feeds thermodynamic hints on cards (Bounty bands). A top-quartile band does not widen treasury split, inflate the positive block, or change what the discoverer earns.

Assurance tiers answer a readability question: how should a stranger label this launch at a glance? An indexer assigns Standard or High from snapshot fields at submit (Assurance tiers). Every audit on live cells shares the same challenge window and claim stake today; the badge is presentation, not a fee schedule. Tiers do not scale discoverer boost, mint size, or pool assignment.

Ripe-slot (deferred) would answer a maintainer / R&D question: where does the next verification primitive want to be built? It ranks vulnerability classes from FMEA + canonization + contagion — beacon only, not on the investor Bob card (Ripe-slot indicator). It does not mint, fund, or gate audits; errors misdirect attention, not money.

The pattern is fixed: settlement moves AUDIT; signal orients attention.

Integrator discipline

Integrators bridge chain facts to human cards. The failure mode is backward: a tier badge becomes an APY input, a top-quartile band becomes a payout multiplier, a High label becomes a shorter window. The contract ignores all three.

Read settlement from chain. Mint previews come from nextPositiveBlockReward() (The positive block); discoverer capacity from escrow balances (Escrow architecture). Pass locked bounty and cohort distribution into card hints so bands read as where this row sits among peers — not as treasury routing. On investor-facing surfaces, show tier disclaimers: informational only; same windows and stakes for every audit today.

Do not back-solve economics from signals. No yield models from tier badges. No payout math that treats top_quartile as an input. No copy that implies faster challenge or lighter stake because the card says High.
